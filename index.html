March saw a significant increase in dispute volume, with 207 disputes opened compared to 81 in February and 93 in January. This represents a 40% dispute rate of total tickets, the highest in the past three months. The most common reason for disputes continues to be "transaction not recognised," accounting for 373 cases (72.0%), which is a typical indication of card fraud in corporate card environments.

Currently, 184 disputes (88.9%) are in progress, representing $50,951 "to be recovered" in credits issued. This is normal for the current month as network resolution times can take 45-120 days, and the recovery of these credits depends on the outcomes of the chargeback processes. Merchant refunds secured $1,268, which is a positive outcome as these are resolved faster than chargebacks.

Our BPS has decreased significantly to 0.13 bps, indicating that, despite the increased volume, disputed amounts are proportionally lower relative to total transaction volume compared to previous months. This is a significant improvement from January (2.29 bps) and February (1.42 bps). The confirmed financial loss is minimal at $32, representing only 0.1% of the credits issued.

Given the high volume of "transaction not recognised" disputes, consider analyzing the specific merchants and cardholders most frequently affected to identify potential patterns of compromised card usage that could be mitigated with enhanced security measures.

Section 8 · Glossary
Key terms explained
Chargeback
A forced reversal of a card transaction initiated by the card network on behalf of the cardholder. The merchant is debited and the issuer (Jeeves) recovers funds.
Win Rate
Percentage of filed disputes where the card network ruled in our favour. Calculated over opened disputes only — not over all tickets.
Temporary Credit
A provisional credit given to the client while the dispute is pending. Reversed if the dispute is lost; becomes permanent if won.
Financial Loss
A credit issued on a case that ended in a non-winning status (lost, not eligible, stopped, below $15). Confirmed cost to Jeeves.
BPS (Basis Points)
Disputed amount as a fraction of total transaction volume, × 10,000. E.g., 5 bps = 0.05% of volume disputed. Processors monitor this metric.
2nd Presentment
A second round of the dispute filed after reviewing the merchant's evidence rebuttal. Last chance before Pre-Arbitration.
Pre-Arbitration
An escalation stage where the card network acts as arbitrator. Higher fees apply. Used when both parties maintain their position after 2nd presentment.
3DS (3D Secure)
Authentication protocol (Verified by Visa / Mastercard SecureCode). If authenticated, fraud liability shifts to the merchant — making chargebacks very hard to file.
Not Eligible
Transaction cannot be disputed via the card network — often because it exceeded the 120-day dispute window or falls under a non-disputable transaction type.
MCC (Merchant Category Code)
4-digit code assigned by card networks to classify merchant type. E.g., 4121 = Taxicabs/Rideshare, 7372 = Software/SaaS. Used to spot dispute concentration by industry.
Stopped
Dispute was intentionally not continued after reviewing merchant evidence — typically when the evidence was compelling enough to justify stopping the process.
Refunded by Merchant
Merchant returned funds voluntarily (without a network ruling). Faster than a chargeback win and avoids dispute fees. Counted as a recovery.
